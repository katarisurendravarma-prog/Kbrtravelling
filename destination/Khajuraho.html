UNESCO Heritage that celebrates architecture, dance, and devotion

The Khajuraho Temples, built by the Chandela dynasty between the 9th and 12th centuries, are celebrated for their intricate carvings depicting art, music, and sensuality. Once 85 in number, only 20 have survived, mostly found in the Western and Eastern groups.

Legend says the Moon God fathered the dynasty with a beautiful Brahmin woman named Hemvati. Her son Chandravarman built Khajuraho's temples as a tribute to love and human creativity.
