Adventure and Serenity in Auli

The Skiing Capital of India in the heart of the Garhwal Himalayas

🏔️ Nature's Playground in Uttarakhand

Auli, nestled in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand, is a pristine hill town offering breathtaking meadows, snow-covered peaks, and alpine charm. Whether you’re a skier, trekker, or honeymooner, Auli has something for every nature lover and thrill seeker.

❄️ Why Visit Auli?

With views of Nanda Devi and Mana Parvat, Auli transforms into a snowy paradise in winter and a lush haven in summer. From skiing adventures to peaceful getaways, it remains a perfect blend of excitement and serenity.

📍 Top Places to Visit in Auli

  • Auli Artificial Lake – High-altitude beauty and snow-making hub
  • Gurso Bugyal – Trek through alpine meadows with mountain views
  • Joshimath – Sacred winter home of Lord Badrinath
  • Kwani Bugyal – Popular trek offering views of Dunagiri & Nanda Devi
  • Auli Ropeway – One of India’s highest and longest cable rides

🎒 Things to Do in Auli

  • Go skiing on powdery slopes from Dec to Feb
  • Ride the Joshimath-Auli cable car for sweeping Himalayan views
  • Trek to Gurso Bugyal for lush meadows and crisp mountain air
  • Camp under the stars with views of snow-laced peaks
  • Relax and photograph the scenic Auli Lake

📅 Best Time to Visit

Winter (Dec–Feb): Best for skiing (-4°C to 7°C)
Summer (Mar–Jun): Pleasant for sightseeing & trekking (10°C to 20°C)
Monsoon (Jul–Sep): Rainy and risky, but crowd-free
Autumn (Oct–Nov): Ideal for clear skies and photography

🎉 Festivals & Events

  • Auli Winter Carnival – Ski tournaments & folk festivities
  • Ganga Dussehra – Celebrated with devotion in nearby Joshimath

🛤️ How to Reach Auli

By Air: Jolly Grant Airport (Dehradun) – 270 km. Taxi to Joshimath + cable car/drive
By Train: Haridwar Railway Station (273 km). Taxis or buses to Joshimath
By Road: Regular buses & taxis connect Dehradun, Rishikesh, Haridwar to Joshimath

🌄 Places Near Auli

  • Joshimath (16 km) – Temples & trek routes
  • Valley of Flowers – Accessible in monsoon, UNESCO Site
  • Badrinath (60 km) – Sacred Char Dham site
  • Hemkund Sahib – Sikh pilgrimage spot in summer
  • Tapovan – Famous for hot springs & mountain scenery
