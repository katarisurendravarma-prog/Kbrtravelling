Unwind in the Serenity of Marari

Golden sands, calm waters, and coastal culture define Kerala’s peaceful retreat

🌴 Overview of Marari

Marari, located in Kerala, is a serene beachside destination offering tranquility, nature, and a glimpse into traditional coastal life. Its laid-back charm, coconut groves, and Ayurvedic wellness spots make it a perfect escape from city bustle.

✨ Why Visit Marari?

Experience untouched beaches, vibrant fishing villages, soothing Ayurvedic therapies, and rustic charm away from tourist crowds. Marari is all about slow travel, rich culture, and rejuvenation by the sea.

📸 Best Places to Visit

  • Marari Beach – Unspoiled coastline with golden sand and peaceful vibes
  • Mararikulam Shiva Temple – Spiritual site reflecting Kerala architecture
  • Pathiramanal Island – Backwater island ideal for birdwatchers and nature lovers
  • Kumarakom Backwaters – Nearby destination for houseboat cruises and village life
  • Arthunkal Church – Historic site with a vibrant annual coastal festival

⛅ Best Time to Visit

Winter (Oct–Mar): Cool and pleasant – ideal for beach outings
Summer (Mar–May): Warm and humid – fewer crowds
Monsoon (Jun–Sep): Lush and green – Ayurvedic season

🎊 Festivals to Experience

  • Onam Festival – Cultural celebrations across Kerala (Aug–Sep)
  • Mararikulam Temple Festivals – Folk dances and temple rituals

🎒 Things to Do in Marari

  • Relax on Marari Beach at sunrise and sunset
  • Experience Ayurvedic therapies at beachside resorts
  • Take a romantic boat ride through the backwaters
  • Explore fishing villages and try coir-making
  • Enjoy kayaking or surfing with friends

🛬 How to Reach Marari

By Air: Cochin Intl. Airport (75 km) – taxis and buses available
By Train: Alappuzha Railway Station (16 km) – good connectivity
By Road: Kochi (1.5 hrs), Alappuzha (30 mins) – smooth drive or bus

📍 Places to Visit Nearby

  • Alappuzha – Backwaters and houseboat experiences
  • Kumarakom – Bird sanctuary and lake views
  • Kochi – Colonial charm, cafes, and seaside forts
  • Krishnapuram Palace – Traditional murals and Kerala heritage
