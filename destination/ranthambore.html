Into the Wild at Ranthambore

Experience tiger safaris, ancient forts, and the raw beauty of Rajasthan's wilderness

🌿 Overview of Ranthambore

Located in Rajasthan, Ranthambore is a premier destination for wildlife lovers and history buffs alike. From tiger spotting in Ranthambore National Park to exploring the UNESCO-listed Ranthambore Fort, the area blends nature and heritage beautifully.

🐅 Why Visit Ranthambore?

Catch a glimpse of Bengal tigers, wander ancient ruins, enjoy safaris, or photograph exotic birds—Ranthambore is a must-visit destination that brings you closer to nature and history.

🏞️ Best Places to Visit

  • Ranthambore National Park – Wildlife haven known for tiger sightings and safaris
  • Ranthambore Fort – 10th-century fort with sweeping park views
  • Surwal Lake – Birdwatching paradise, especially during winter
  • Kachida Valley – Leopard and bear spotting in hilly terrain
  • Raj Bagh Ruins – Ancient structures nestled in the forest

⛅ Best Time to Visit

Winter (Oct–Mar): Ideal for safaris and sightseeing
Summer (Apr–Jun): Very hot but tigers often spotted near waterholes
Monsoon (Jul–Sep): Park is closed; greenery returns

🎊 Festivals & Events

  • Ranthambore Festival (December) – Celebrates nature, wildlife, and local culture
  • Diwali – Witness the traditional charm of local Rajasthani celebrations

🎒 Things to Do in Ranthambore

  • Take a tiger safari in Ranthambore National Park
  • Explore the historic Ranthambore Fort
  • Birdwatch at Surwal Lake
  • Photograph wildlife in Kachida Valley
  • Enjoy bonfires and camping in nature resorts

🛬 How to Reach Ranthambore

By Air: Jaipur Airport (180 km); taxis and buses available
By Train: Sawai Madhopur Junction (10 km) connects to major cities
By Road: Well-connected to Jaipur, Delhi, and Kota by taxi or bus

📍 Places to Visit Nearby

  • Karauli – Known for temples and havelis (80 km)
  • Bundi – Forts, step-wells, and heritage (110 km)
  • Chauth Mata Temple – Scenic hilltop temple (20 km)
  • Sawai Madhopur – Explore markets and local life (10 km)
