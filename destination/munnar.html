Discover the Serenity of Munnar

Misty hills, tea gardens, and tranquil getaways await you in Kerala’s green jewel

🌿 Overview of Munnar

Munnar is a captivating hill station nestled in Kerala’s Western Ghats, known for its cool climate, lush landscapes, and emerald tea gardens. It's a paradise for nature lovers, trekkers, and honeymooners alike.

✨ Why Visit Munnar?

Whether it's walking through tea plantations, exploring waterfalls, or enjoying the quiet charm of the hills—Munnar offers the perfect blend of relaxation and adventure.

📸 Best Places to Visit

  • Eravikulam National Park – Home to Nilgiri Tahr and Anamudi Peak
  • Mattupetty Dam – Scenic boating and picnic spot
  • Tea Gardens – Endless green beauty and tea tasting
  • Attukal Waterfalls – Ideal for trekking and photography
  • Top Station – Panoramic views of the Western Ghats

⛅ Best Time to Visit

Summer (Mar–May): Pleasant for sightseeing
Monsoon (Jun–Sep): Lush and rainy, great for nature lovers
Winter (Oct–Feb): Cool and perfect for hill holidays

🎊 Festivals to Experience

  • Makaravilakku Festival – Traditional rituals (January)
  • Onam – Colorful Kerala harvest celebrations (Aug/Sep)
  • Tea Festival – Celebrating Munnar’s heritage (October)

🎒 Things to Do in Munnar

  • Explore Eravikulam’s wildlife and hike Anamudi Peak
  • Boat ride at Mattupetty Dam
  • Visit the Tata Tea Museum
  • Drive to Top Station for breathtaking views
  • Trek to Attukal Waterfalls

🛬 How to Reach Munnar

By Air: Cochin International Airport (110 km); taxis and buses available
By Train: Aluva Railway Station (100 km); good rail connectivity
By Road: Scenic drive or public transport from Kochi, Madurai, Coimbatore

📍 Places to Visit Nearby

  • Thekkady – Wildlife sanctuary and spice gardens
  • Alleppey – Backwaters and houseboat experiences
  • Wayanad – Waterfalls, trekking, and tribal culture
  • Chilavannur Lake – Quiet, serene lake escape
