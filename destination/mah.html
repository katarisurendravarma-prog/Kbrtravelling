Mahabalipuram – The Temple City of Tamil Nadu

Where legends meet the ocean's edge

Mahabalipuram, also known as Mamallapuram, is a coastal town located 60 km south of Chennai. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, it's famous for rock-cut temples, cave sanctuaries, and monolithic monuments that date back to the Pallava dynasty of the 7th and 8th centuries.

📅 Best Time to Visit

From November to February when the weather is cool and ideal for sightseeing and outdoor exploration.

🏛️ Top Attractions

  • Shore Temple: 8th-century Pallava marvel by the Bay of Bengal.
  • Arjuna’s Penance: Giant open-air rock relief with mythological scenes.
  • Five Rathas: Monolithic temples shaped like chariots, each carved from a single stone.
  • Krishna’s Butterball: A giant granite boulder balanced on a slope for over 1,200 years.
  • Mamallapuram Lighthouse: Offering panoramic coastal views from above.

🎉 Things to Do

  • Attend the Mamallapuram Dance Festival: Held December–January, showcasing Indian classical dances near Arjuna’s Penance.
  • Explore Indian Seashell Museum: A unique place with rare shells and marine artifacts.
  • Visit Crocodile Bank: Just 14 km away, home to over 2,500 crocodiles.
  • Relax on the beach: Mahabalipuram Beach and Wide Beach are perfect for a sunset walk.
  • Alamparai Fort: A Mughal-era seaside fort with historic ruins.

🚗 How to Reach

By Road: 57 km from Chennai, well connected by bus and taxi.

🚉 Nearest Railway Station

Chengalpattu – 29 km away with good road links.

✈️ Nearest Airport

Chennai International Airport – around 60 km from Mahabalipuram.
