Magical Hills of Shillong

Explore waterfalls, canyons, lakes, music, and Meghalaya's living culture

🌿 Overview of Shillong

Shillong, nestled in the Khasi hills of Meghalaya, enchants visitors with pine forests, cascading waterfalls, colonial charm, and a rich musical heritage. Dubbed the "Scotland of the East," this hill town is a vibrant mix of nature and culture.

🎶 Why Visit Shillong?

From trekking in Laitlum to boating on Umiam Lake, shopping in Police Bazaar, and attending Cherry Blossom festivals—Shillong is a complete package for adventurers, romantics, and culture lovers alike.

🏞️ Best Places to Visit

  • Umiam Lake – Peaceful reservoir ideal for boating and picnics
  • Shillong Peak – Highest viewpoint with Himalayan vistas
  • Elephant Falls – Popular multi-tiered waterfall near the city
  • Ward's Lake – Central city lake with paddle boating and gardens
  • Don Bosco Museum – Cultural hub of Northeast India
  • Laitlum Canyons – Scenic gorges for hikers and photographers
  • Police Bazaar – Vibrant shopping and street food experience

⛅ Best Time to Visit

Spring (Mar–May): Flower blooms and great weather
Monsoon (Jun–Aug): Lush greenery and dramatic landscapes
Autumn–Winter (Sep–Feb): Clear skies, festivals, and cool air

🎉 Festivals & Events

  • Cherry Blossom Festival (Nov) – Music, food, and pink blooms
  • Shad Suk Mynsiem – Traditional Khasi spring thanksgiving dance

🎒 Things to Do in Shillong

  • Trek to Laitlum Canyons
  • Boat on Umiam Lake
  • Attend the Cherry Blossom Festival
  • Shop for local crafts in Police Bazaar
  • Chill in Shillong's cozy cafes with live music

🛬 How to Reach Shillong

By Air: Shillong Airport (30 km) or Guwahati Airport (125 km)
By Train: Guwahati Railway Station, 3–4 hours by road
By Road: Cabs, shared jeeps, or buses from Guwahati and NE states

📍 Places to Visit Nearby

  • Cherrapunji – Famous for root bridges and waterfalls (54 km)
  • Mawlynnong – Asia's cleanest village (78 km)
  • Dawki & Umngot River – Boating on crystal waters (95 km)
  • Mawsynram – Wettest place on Earth with caves (61 km)
  • Nongriat – Scenic treks and double-decker root bridges
