Nubra Valley – The Cold Desert of Ladakh

Snow peaks, sand dunes, and spiritual sanctity await

Nestled in the northeast of Ladakh, Nubra Valley is known for its unique cold desert landscapes, Buddhist monasteries, and tranquil charm. Located at an altitude of 3,048 m, it is one of the driest and most magical places in the Indian Himalayas.

📅 Best Time to Visit

Visit from May to September for warmth and access. February attracts visitors with the colorful Dosmoche Festival.

🌄 Top Places to Visit

Panamik Village

The last village before the Indo-Tibetan border, known for hot springs and Himalayan serenity.

Yarab Tso (Hidden Lake)

A sacred alpine lake near Sumur, accessed by a peaceful uphill trek.

Maitreya Buddha at Diskit

A majestic 32-meter statue overlooking the valley, symbolizing peace and protection.

🐫 Things to Do in Nubra

  • Ride a Bactrian camel at Hunder’s cold desert
  • Bathe in Panamik’s sulphur hot springs
  • Buy local crafts and Pashmina shawls from local markets

🚗 How to Reach

Reach Nubra via Khardung La Pass, one of the world’s highest motorable roads. Diskit is 150 km from Leh.

🚉 Nearest Railway Station

Udhampur Railway Station – approx. 298 km away.

✈️ Nearest Airport

Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport – about 73 km from Nubra.
