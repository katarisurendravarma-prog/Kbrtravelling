Leh – The Land of High Passes

Explore the rugged charm and mystical beauty of Ladakh

Located at an altitude of 3,524 meters above sea level in Ladakh, Leh is surrounded by rugged mountains and pristine beauty. From Buddhist monasteries and blue lakes to mystical magnetic hills and camel safaris – Leh is a land of wonder and high-altitude adventures.

📅 Best Time to Visit

Visit Leh from May to August for the best weather. September is also wonderful due to the colorful Ladakh Festival.

📍 Top Places to Visit

  • Leh Palace: 17th-century royal residence with panoramic views.
  • Hemis Monastery: The largest and wealthiest monastery in Ladakh.
  • Thiksey Monastery: Known for its 49ft Maitreya Buddha statue.
  • Shey Palace & Monastery: Features a giant copper Buddha statue.
  • Shanti Stupa: A peaceful dome structure with panoramic views.

🎒 Things to Do in Leh

  • Marvel at the crystal-blue Pangong Lake
  • Visit Tso Moriri, India’s highest lake
  • Defy gravity at Magnetic Hill
  • Experience the vibrant Chham Dance during Ladakh Festival
  • Ride a Bactrian camel in Nubra Valley

🚗 How to Reach

By Road: 418 km from Srinagar, 473 km from Manali with scenic mountain routes.

🚉 Nearest Railway Station

Jammu Tawi – about 712 km from Leh.

✈️ Nearest Airport

Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport with flights from Delhi, Mumbai, and Srinagar.
